## Question

Corporate systems rely on clearly defined roles and authority to resolve 
deadlock, where managers or higher levels in the chain of command can make 
final decisions even if there is disagreement. In contrast, open source 
projects tend to rely on consensus and, when necessary, voting among 
contributors to move forward. How do these different approaches to 
resolving disagreement compare in terms of how quickly decisions are made, 
how fair participants perceive the process to be, and the overall quality 
of the outcomes that result?
