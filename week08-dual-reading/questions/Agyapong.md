To what extent do managers in hierarchical organizations use their employees as proxies in conflicts they are unwilling or unable to fight themselves? This could be sending an employee to argue for more resources from another department, having a subordinate surface a complaint the manager is too politically exposed to raise, or channeling general dissatisfaction downward so that the employee becomes the target/origin of the complaint? The book warns employees about the dangers of bypassing the chain of command, but what about the reverse, when the chain of command bypasses itself through us, the newer/lower employees.

How could an employee recognize when they are being positioned as a proxy in someone else's fight, and how can the open-source model's reliance on transparent, public deliberation (where a BD's influence is checked by 'forkability' and other open source principle, allowing everyone can see who everyone to see who is advocating for what) be applied or used when it comes to personal relations and conversations in an organization?
